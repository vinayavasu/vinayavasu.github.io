05. speaking

Conference talks

Talk Accepted
The Diana Initiative  ·  dianainitiative.org
Before You Hack It, Understand It: AI Security Fundamentals
📅 30 May 2026
🌐 Online
🕐 Pacific Daylight Time (UTC-07:00)
As security practitioners, we are being handed AI targets to audit, yet many of us still treat the model itself as a black box. We are expected to pen-test and defend these systems, but how do you find an injection point if you do not know what a token is? How do you assess supply chain risk without understanding model artifacts?

This talk covers foundational neural network and LLM architecture through a security lens, not a data science one. Starting with vectors and embeddings, we build up through tokenization, attention mechanisms, and system prompts — connecting each concept directly to its attack surface. We then show where real attacks live in the stack: prompt injection, data poisoning, RAG pipeline corruption, supply chain compromise, and inference abuse, each mapped to the exact architectural layer it targets.

The goal is to give practitioners the mental model to approach AI systems like any other target — with a real understanding of what is underneath. Ultimately, you cannot hack, defend, or audit what you do not understand.
